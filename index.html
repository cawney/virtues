Explaining 13 Virtues

Ever since I read Benjamin Franklin's biography, I've really been fascinated with his 13 virtues. Every day he tried to practice these 13 virtues. If he felt he lived up to his reputation for each one for that day, he would put a black dot on that day and virtue. The goal was to fill his calendar with black dots that corresponded with his virtues.

These are his 13 virtues, but hopefully I'll learn enough to figure out a way for this to make it my own and change the virtues to something personal to me.

Temperence
Do everything in moderation
Silence
Pleade the 5th
Order
Let everything you own have a place and each part of your business have it's time.
Resolution
Resolve to perform what you ought; perform without fail what you resolve.
Frugality
Waste nothing.
Industry
Lose no time, always be employeed in something useful.
Sincerity
Be honest with your words, but also kind.
Justice
Don't bring harm to others for your benefit.
Moderation
Avoid extremes.
Cleanliness
Tolerate no uncleanliness in body, cloaths or habitation
Tranquility
Do not be disturbed by trifles
Chastity
Don't use sex as a distraction
Humility
Immitate Jesus and Socrates
